How Often Should You Sealcoat a Commercial Parking Lot?

How often to sealcoat a commercial asphalt lot, what affects the schedule, and why timing protects your pavement investment.

Sealcoating is the cheapest thing you can do to protect an expensive asset. But sealcoat too often and you're wasting money; wait too long and you're paying for repairs instead. So what's the right schedule?

The general rule

Most commercial asphalt lots should be sealcoated every 2 to 3 years. New asphalt should cure for several months — typically a full season — before its first coat.

What changes the timing

Heavy traffic, lots of turning movements, fuel and oil drips, and full sun exposure all wear sealer faster. A busy retail lot or a warehouse with truck traffic may need it closer to every two years, while a low-traffic church lot can often stretch to three. The honest test: if the surface has faded from black to gray and water no longer beads, it's time.

Always crack-seal first

Sealcoat is a surface protectant, not a crack filler. Cracks must be cleaned and sealed before sealcoating so water can't reach the base. Skipping that step lets damage keep spreading under a fresh-looking coat.

Plan it into a maintenance program

The properties that spend the least over 20 years are the ones on a plan — scheduled crack sealing, sealcoating and striping that keep small problems small. We build those plans for churches, schools, HOAs and commercial owners across our service area.
