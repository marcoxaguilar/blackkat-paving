ADA Parking Lot Striping Requirements, Explained

A plain-English guide to ADA accessible parking — how many spaces you need, access aisles, van spaces and signage for commercial lots.

ADA accessible parking isn't optional, and getting it wrong exposes a property to complaints and liability. Here's a plain-English overview for owners and managers. (This is general guidance — your local code and the current ADA standards govern your specific lot.)

How many accessible spaces?

The number scales with total stall count. As a rough guide, a lot up to 25 spaces needs at least one accessible space; the requirement increases from there. A share of accessible spaces must also be van-accessible.

Access aisles

Every accessible space needs a marked access aisle beside it so a wheelchair or lift can deploy. Van spaces need a wider aisle. The aisle must be level and connect to an accessible route to the building entrance.

Signage and markings

Accessible spaces require the proper symbol painted on the pavement and a mounted sign at the correct height, with van spaces marked accordingly. Faded or missing signage is one of the most common — and easily fixed — violations.

Get it laid out right

When we re-stripe or lay out a new lot, we plan accessible spaces, aisles, routes and signage to current standards as part of the job. If you're unsure whether your lot complies, we'll flag it during a free assessment.
